Projectile Motion

Projectile motion is a form of motion where an object is thrown or projected into

In 1600s, armies used equations of motions to calculate velocities and angle for firing a missile to hit a target. While a quarterback does not do explicit calculations using equations of motion, a computerised footbal game will certainly need to do these calculations to determine the trajectory of the ball. In this project, we will write a program to calculate the trajectory of a projectile and determine if it hits a target. We will use the equations of motion to calculate the position of the projectile at any given time and check if it intersects with the target.



We will use the metric notations that are favored for all scientific and engineering calculations. The distance will be measured in meters (m), the time in seconds (s), and the velocity in meters per second (m/s). The acceleration due to gravity will be denoted as g, which is approximately 9.81 m/s² on Earth.

Abbreviations

  • meters, m
  • kilometers, km: 1000 meters make up a kilometer
  • meters per second, m/s: units for measuring distance
  • meters per second squared, m/s²: units for measuring acceleration
